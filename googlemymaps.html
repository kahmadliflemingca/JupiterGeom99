Google My Maps

Google My Maps is a web-based mapping platform created by Google that allows users to create, customize and share maps made from their own data. Google My maps allows users to create custom maps from uploaded data in KML, XLSX or CSV for vector space data. The map can then be customized using a variety of colours, icons and text options. The final format is stored on the users Google Drive and can be shared collaboratively to other Google users Drives. Sharing is also available via link, which can be made private.

+

  • Pros

  • Free
  • Easy to Use, Update and Navigate
  • Displays Multiple Data Layers
  • Display Attribute Data Onclick
  • Leverages Google Base Maps

  • Cons

  • Very Basic Interface, Lacks Functionality
  • Does Not Accept Large Amounts of Data (10MB)
  • No Advanced Querying
  • Difficulty Displaying Multiple Overlapping Data
  • Accepts Limited File Types (KML, CSV, XLSX)

What Didn't Work

The size limit for KML and XLSX files is 10 MB, while the size limit for CSV files is 5 MB. Keep in mind that the actual limit may be lower depending on the complexity of the data. In addition to the file size limits, there are also limits on the number of points and lines you can add to your map. Google My Maps allows up to 10,000 points, lines, or shapes per layer, and up to 10 layers per map.

What Did Work

Google My Maps is a great tool for creating custom maps with your own data. It does a particularly good job of displaying relatively small, simple datasets in a secure way that can be collaborative or shared privately. I had great success creating a small map with a few simple layers and sharing it collaboratively with a colleague, privately and publicly.
